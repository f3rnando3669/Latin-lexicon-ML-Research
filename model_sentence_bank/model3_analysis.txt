Passage Analysis:
1. "There is a time in every man's education when he arrives at the conviction that envy is
ignorance; that imitation is suicide; that he must take himself for better for worse as his
portion; that though the wide universe is full of good, no kernel of nourishing corn can
come to him but through his toil bestowed on that plot of ground which is given to him to
till." (CXC-D-CV-P-A-ST-M)
2. "That power which resides in him is new in nature, and none but he knows what that is
which he can do, nor does he know until he has tried." (CC-D-RB-L-ST)
3. "Not for nothing one face, one character, one fact, makes much impression on him and
another none." (S-D-JA-A-ST)
4. "This sculpture in the memory is not without pre-established harmony." (S-D-SM-L-ST)
5. "The eye was placed where one ray should fall, that it might testify of that particular ray."
(CC-D-RB-L-ST)
6. "We but half express ourselves, and are ashamed of that divine idea which each of us
represents." (C-D-RB-L-ST)
7. "It may be safely trusted as proportionate and of good issues, so it be faithfully imparted,
but God will not have his work made manifest by cowards." (CXC-D-RB-L-A-ST-M)
8. "A man is relieved and gay when he has put his heart into his work and done his best; but
what he has said or done otherwise shall give him no peace." (CXC-D-CV-L-A-ST)
9. "It is a deliverance which does not deliver." (S-D-JA-A-ST)
10. "In the attempt his genius deserts him; no muse befriends; no invention, no hope." (C-D-JA-A-ST)
