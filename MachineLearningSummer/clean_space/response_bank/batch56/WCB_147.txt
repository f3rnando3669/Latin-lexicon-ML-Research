"Every time my sister uses hairspray, it is an extremely hot day" is an example of <WCB>, Insufficient Causal Basis. This type of defective argument occurs when a causal relationship is assumed between two events without sufficient evidence to support such a connection. In this case, the statement suggests a causal link between using hairspray and the occurrence of extremely hot days, but no adequate evidence is provided to establish this causality.
