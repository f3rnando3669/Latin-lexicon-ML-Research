The argument "Idea X is popular. Therefore, X is correct." is an example of a defective argument type. This particular argument can be classified under <FU>, which is the Presumptuous Consensus fallacy. 

### Breakdown of the Argument:
- **Form:** Idea X is popular.
- **Conclusion:** Therefore, X is correct.

### Defective Argument Type:
- **<FU> Presumptuous Consensus:**
  This argument assumes that the popularity of an idea is a sufficient proof of its correctness. This is flawed because just because an idea is widely accepted does not necessarily mean it is true. Popular opinion can be wrong or based on insufficient evidence.

### Conclusion:
The argument "Idea X is popular. Therefore, X is correct." falls into the category <FU> Presumptuous Consensus, illustrating the fallacy of assuming that popularity equates to truth.
