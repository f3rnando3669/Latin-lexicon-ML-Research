The statement "This makes you think you need to believe or buy something because everyone else is." points to a technique that relies on the idea that one should conform to what the majority is doing or thinking. This defective argument is an example of the **Bandwagon Fallacy**, which can be categorized under the defective argument type <FU>: Presumptuous Consensus.

**<FU> Presumptuous Consensus** refers to arguments that assume a truth or a course of action is correct simply because a majority of people believe it to be so or are following it. This kind of reasoning is faulty because the popularity of an idea does not necessarily guarantee its validity or efficacy.
