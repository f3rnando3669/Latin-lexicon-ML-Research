< IDAA > stands for Irrelevant Distraction from Another Argument. In this context, the argument "Why worry about taking attendance when there are so many starving children in the world?" presents itself as a type of red herring fallacy, drawing attention away from the issue at hand (taking attendance) by introducing another concern (starving children) that, although significant, is not directly related to the initial topic.

Thus, the argument distracts from addressing the reasons or importance of taking attendance by shifting the focus to an entirely different and unrelated issue.
