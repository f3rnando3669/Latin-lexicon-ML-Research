The argument presented in the example can be identified as falling under the defective argument type <FU>, which stands for "Presumptuous Consensus". This logical fallacy occurs when someone assumes a proposition is true primarily because many other people believe it to be true, without examining the actual evidence or rationale behind it.

In this case, Marie decides that a low-carb diet and drinking protein shakes must be the healthy way to eat simply because many of her friends are doing it. She presumes the consensus among her friends equates to a factual or healthy conclusion, disregarding the need for independent, empirical investigation.
