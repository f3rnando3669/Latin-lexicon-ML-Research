Applying the <IDAA> (Identify Defective Argument Application) to the statement:

"If the President arrives in Hawaii, and the next day a volcano erupts, believing that the volcano erupted because the President arrived on the island is an example of:"

This statement is an example of the defective argument type called Insufficient Causal Basis (<WCB>). This is because it asserts a causal relationship between the President's arrival and the volcanic eruption without sufficient evidence to support that causation. The assumption that the President's arrival caused the volcano to erupt is based on a coincidental occurrence rather than a proven causal connection.
