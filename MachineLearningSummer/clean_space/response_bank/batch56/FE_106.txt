The argument "Either you are for us, or you're against us!" is an example of a false dichotomy, which is a specific type of defective argument. This kind of argument presents two options as the only possibilities when, in reality, other alternatives may exist.

However, based on the defective argument types you've defined, the one that most closely aligns with this argument is:

<IR> Inconclusive Reason.

The argument is presenting an oversimplified and incomplete reasoning by excluding other potential positions or viewpoints that might exist. By doing so, it forces a binary choice that does not necessarily represent the full spectrum of possible stances on the issue.
