The statement "If you eat a good diet, you will never have a heart attack." is an example of a defective argument known as Inconclusive Reason (<IR>). This is because it makes a broad claim without considering other factors that contribute to heart health, such as genetics, lifestyle choices, and other medical conditions. The reasoning is not sufficiently conclusive to support the absolute statement.
