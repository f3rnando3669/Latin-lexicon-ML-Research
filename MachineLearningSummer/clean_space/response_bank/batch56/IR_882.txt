<IDAA>, or Irrelevant Direct Appeal to Adverse Appeal, is used in the statement "You can’t give me a C. I’ll lose my scholarship."

Explanation: The statement attempts to appeal to the personal consequences of receiving a C grade (losing the scholarship) rather than addressing the academic merit or reason behind the grade itself. The argument is defective because it relies on the potential adverse outcome for the student rather than focusing on whether the grade is justified based on their academic performance.
