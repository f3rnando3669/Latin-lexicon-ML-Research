The argument "My cat sleeps all day, so cats must be pretty lazy." illustrates a <G>, or Generalization.

Here, a conclusion about all cats is drawn based on the behavior of a single cat. This is a classic example of making a general statement from a limited set of observations, leading to a potentially faulty or oversimplified conclusion.
