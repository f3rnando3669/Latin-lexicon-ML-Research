The argument presented is an example of an ad hominem fallacy, where the opponent attacks the character or personal habits of the proponent rather than addressing the argument itself. In your list of defective argument types, this closely aligns with Inconclusive Reason (IR), even though it's specifically an ad hominem, which is a kind of irrelevant attack or diversion tactic to undermine the argument without providing a substantial counterargument. 

Thus, applying the categorization:

**<IR>**: "My opponent suggests that lowering taxes will be a good idea, this is coming from a woman who eats a pint of Ben and Jerry's each night!"
