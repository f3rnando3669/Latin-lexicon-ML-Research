- **Read the Argument Carefully:** The passage presents a conversation between Alice and the king. Alice states she sees "nobody" on the road. The king humorously interprets "nobody" as if it were a visible person.
- **Identify the Propositional Content:** Alice’s statement is that she sees no one. The king's response twists the meaning humorously, suggesting that seeing "Nobody" (as if it were an entity) is a special ability.

- **Check for Weak Causal Basis (WCB):** No cause-and-effect reasoning is presented.
- **Check for False Universals (FU):** There is no reliance on unproven, universally agreed points.
- **Check for Double-Edged Phrases (DEP):** The passage involves dual interpretations of the word "nobody," which can be seen both as literally nobody (meaning no person is present) and as if 'nobody' were a person to be seen.

- **Categorize the Argument:** Double-Edged Phrases (DEP)
