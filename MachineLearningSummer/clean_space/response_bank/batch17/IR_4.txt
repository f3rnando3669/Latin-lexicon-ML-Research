### Step 1: Understand the Defective Argument Types
Ensure a clear understanding of each defective argument type (<G>, <FE>, <IR>, <RR>, <WCB>, <FU>, <DEP>).

1. **<G> Generalization**: Claiming a general truth based on a specific instance.
2. **<FE> False Enumeration**: Listing fewer or more options than actually exist.
3. **<IR> Inappropriate Reason**: Providing a reason that doesn't logically support the proposition.
4. **<RR> Redundant Reason**: New reason appears to be presented but essentially repeats the proposition.
5. **<WCB> Weak Causal Basis**: Offering a causal basis that is not compelling enough.
6. **<FU> False Universals**: Asserting universally agreed points without proof.
7. **<DEP> Double-Edged Phrases**: Using statements open to dual interpretations.
